Zion

About

Utah’s first and most visited national park, renowned for its towering sandstone cliffs, deep canyons, and lush river valleys. The Virgin River carves through the dramatic landscape, creating stunning features like Zion Canyon, The Narrows, and Emerald Pools. The park is a hiker’s paradise, with iconic trails such as Angel’s Landing and Observation Point offering breathtaking views. Zion’s diverse ecosystems range from desert landscapes to waterfalls and hanging gardens, making it a haven for wildlife and adventure seekers. With its awe-inspiring scenery and world-class hiking, Zion is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

Trails

Angel's Landing Trail

One of the most famous and thrilling hikes in the world, this 5.4-mile round-trip trail features a steep ascent, sheer drop-offs, and a narrow ridge with chains for support. The breathtaking views from the summit make the strenuous climb well worth the challenge. A permit is required to hike the final section..

Emerald Pools Trail

A family-friendly hike that leads to beautiful waterfalls and pools, this trail has three sections: Lower (1.2 miles round-trip), Middle (2 miles round-trip), and Upper (3 miles round-trip). The scenic walk features lush vegetation and stunning rock formations, making it a great option for all ages.

Pa'rus Trail

Is a scenic, paved 3.5-mile round-trip path along the Virgin River, offering stunning red rock views. Ideal for all skill levels, it's wheelchair and pet-friendly, making it perfect for a leisurely walk or bike ride. Enjoy peaceful nature, wildlife, and breathtaking sunsets along the way.

Riverside Walk

This scenic 2.2-mile round-trip trail that follows the Virgin River, leading to the famous Narrows. This easy, paved path winds through towering canyon walls, lush vegetation, and serene river views. Ideal for all ages, it offers shade, wildlife sightings, and peaceful spots to enjoy the beauty of Zion before reaching the river entry point for The Narrows hike.

Watchman's Trail

This a moderate 3.3-mile round-trip hike that climbs 368 feet, offering breathtaking views of the Watchman peak, Virgin River, and Springdale. Featuring scenic switchbacks and desert flora, this lesser-traveled trail provides a peaceful escape with stunning canyon overlooks.
