Zion

About

Utah's first and most visited national park, renowned for its towering sandstone cliffs, deep canyons, and lush river valleys. The Virgin River carves through the dramatic landscape, creating stunning features like Zion Canyon, The Narrows, and Emerald Pools. The park is a hiker's paradise, with iconic trails such as Angel's Landing and Observation Point offering breathtaking views. Zion's diverse ecosystems range from desert landscapes to waterfalls and hanging gardens, making it a haven for wildlife and adventure seekers. With its awe-inspiring scenery and world-class hiking, Zion is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

Trails

Angel's Landing Trail

One of the most famous and thrilling hikes in the world, this 5.4-mile round-trip trail features a steep ascent, sheer drop-offs, and a narrow ridge with chains for support. The breathtaking views from the summit make the strenuous climb well worth the challenge.

Canyon Overlook

The Canyon Overlook Trail is a moderate 1-mile (1.6 km) hike, featuring sandstone steps and rocky terrain that culminates in stunning views, if you get lucky you might even see a bighorn sheep.

Pa'rus Trail

The Pa'rus Trail, meaning "bubbling water," offers views of the Watchman along the Virgin River. This paved, accessible trail is perfect for sunsets, stargazing, and astrophotography.

Taylor Creek

The Taylor Creek Trail takes hikers through a narrow box canyon to the Double Arch Alcove and historic homestead cabins, many parts of the canyon never receive sunlight so be prepared for cold temperatures.

360 Virtual Tour Extra Content

Watchman's Trail

The Watchman Trail is a moderate out-and-back hike starting from the Zion Canyon Visitor Center. The trail begins flat before climbing to a stunning viewpoint of the Watchman, Temples and Towers, and lower Zion Canyon.
