Arches

About

Known for its more than 2,000 natural sandstone arches, towering pinnacles, and dramatic rock formations. Spanning over 76,000 acres, the park showcases the unique effects of erosion, with iconic landmarks like Delicate Arch, Landscape Arch, and Balanced Rock drawing visitors from around the world.

Trails

Delicate Arch

This iconic 3-mile hike leads to the breathtaking Delicate Arch, Utah's most famous landmark. The moderate-to-strenuous trail includes a steady incline, slick rock sections, and sweeping panoramic views, rewarding hikers with an unforgettable sight.

Devils Garden

Devils Garden is a must-see destination for adventurers. This area boasts a stunning array of natural wonders, including towering spires, arches, and delicate fins (narrow rock walls formed by water erosion).

360 Virtual Tour

Double Arch

Double Arch, located in The Windows section of Arches National Park, is a remarkable natural formation, being both the tallest (112 feet) and second-longest (144 feet) arch within the park. It offers a quick and rewarding experience for all visitors.

360 Virtual Tour

Landscape Arch Trail

A relatively easy 1.6-mile round-trip hike, this trail leads to Landscape Arch, one of the longest natural arches in the world. The thin, fragile-looking arch stretches 290 feet and is a testament to the park's ongoing geological transformations.

360 Virtual Tour

Windows Loop Trail

This easy 1-mile loop takes hikers to some of the park's most accessible and picturesque arches, including the North and South Windows and Turret Arch. The short trek offers spectacular views, especially at sunrise and sunset.
