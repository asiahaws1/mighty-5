Bryce Canyon

About

Renowned for its striking landscape of hoodoos—tall, thin rock spires formed by erosion. Located in southern Utah, the park's high elevation (up to 9,100 feet) offers cooler temperatures and sweeping views of its massive natural amphitheaters.The park is also a designated Dark Sky Park, making it an incredible spot for stargazing.

Trails

Bryce Point to Inspiration Point

This 2.9-mile out-and-back section of the Rim Trail follows the edge of the Bryce Amphitheater, offering spectacular panoramic views. It's an excellent option for those looking for an easier hike while still enjoying the park's signature landscapes.

Fairyland Loop

At 8 miles, this strenuous but less crowded hike offers stunning views of Fairyland Canyon's dramatic hoodoos and rock fins. Hikers enjoy breathtaking scenery, including Tower Bridge, while experiencing a more secluded part of the park..

Navajo Loop

This popular 1.3-mile loop descends into Bryce Amphitheater, winding through hoodoos, slot canyons, and the famed Wall Street section, with highlights like Thor's Hammer.

Queens Garden Trail

This 1.8-mile trail, often paired with the Navajo Loop, descends into a hoodoo-filled canyon, featuring the Queen Victoria rock formation.
